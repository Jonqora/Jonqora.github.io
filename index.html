About Me

Confident speaker, skilled writer, and passionate teacher: I love to solve challenging problems and to learn about almost anything.

My broad interests lacked direction at first. I drifted through two degrees this way, aimlessly chomping knowledge like a PAC-MAN without any certainty of where I should end up.

A struggle to complete my master's thesis pushed me to finally identify what workflow and skills to look for in a well-matched career. My analysis pointed to software development, reigniting long-held interests that I had only before seen as a hobby. I am now enrolled in a CS degree program at UBC, working to master the foundational skills I'll need to succeed.

From previous work I hold a BSc in psychology and a research-based MSc in marketing. My thesis developed a new theory for understanding non-listening interactions in high-conflict settings; a summary is linked on this page.

I aspire to design, write and maintain code for innovative software projects that improve lives. My psychology background gives me a strong user-centered focus, and I posess a high standard of excellence and dedication to learning. I aspire to join and work with tech organizations who share these values.
